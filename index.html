Panic! At the Disco

Brendon Urie of Panic! At the Disco. Provided by Janey Z on Flickr.

American rock band, Panic! At the Disco was formed in 2004 by high school friends Brendon Urie, Ryan Ross and Spencer Smith. After sending a sample of their music to Fall Out Boy, the band was signed to the label Decaydance Records. Their debut album A Fever You Can't Sweat Out initially had slow record sales, but after the release of their first sigle "I Write Sins Not Tragedies" the band gained popularity. Although two of the founding members, Ross and Smith, are no longer with the band, Panic! At the Disco has maintained popularity, releasing five albums within the past ten years, with a sixth album, Death of a Bachelor, set to release in 2016.
